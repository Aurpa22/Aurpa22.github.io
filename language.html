Professional Proficiency

Professional proficiency indicates the ability to:

  • Communicate effectively in most formal and informal situations
  • Participate fully in professional discussions and meetings
  • Write detailed reports and professional documents
  • Understand complex texts and technical materials
  • Use the language flexibly and effectively for social and professional purposes

This level corresponds to C1 on the Common European Framework of Reference for Languages (CEFR).

×

Native Proficiency

Native proficiency indicates:

  • The language was acquired in early childhood
  • Complete mastery of the language in all contexts
  • Ability to understand and produce complex, nuanced language
  • Intuitive understanding of cultural references and idioms
  • Ability to use the language for sophisticated academic and professional purposes

This is the highest level of language proficiency, equivalent to that of an educated native speaker.
